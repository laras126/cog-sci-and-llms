From day 1, we will start collecting qualitative data to better understand our own LLM use. Through this study, we can find themes on what use cases are successful or not successful, and build the habit of reflecting on our LLM use. You will design another study in week 5 to get more insight into themes emerging from this study.

**Active:** Weeks 1–5 (optional to continue until end of seminar - could be a compliment to your individual study)

## Overview

In this study, we will record a "diary entry" at the beginning and end of each workday. At the beginning of the workday, you will reflect on how you think you'll use LLMs throughout the day -- for work tasks and non work tasks. The data collected will be private to you, other than reporting on your ultimate findings based on the aggregate data. 

You will add an entry each AM and PM every workday, and we will set up an email reminder with a link to the form. I recommend before logging the PM entry, to review the LLM chat history from the day to jog your memory.

Here is an example reflection from one of my entries from a single day:

- **AM:** "may get/confirm some ideas about analysis and how to not redo it, probably get feedback on some emails and ask some running questions, also get feedback on or do some content organizing for course plans"
- **PM:** "Successful use for getting me to do bluesky and linkedin posts - again i think this is offloading metacognition, like what to do next, rather than offloading the doing the work - e.g., it didn't write anything for me, but gave feedback and said what to do next.. also used claude code when working on the TOCE notebook and i actually think that may have slowed things down, and i found some made up variables, so had to fix it in places (or have it fix itself). i think for quick few line changes, it just takes longer because it has to read all the text with bash commands"

There is no right/wrong length to these - mine vary from around 30ish words to 150ish words. Some days will be short, some will be longer. The most important thing is to take the time to do them.

## Steps

1. Create a form (e.g., MS forms or Google forms) for data collection with two paragraph text fields named:
    1. AM: Think about your tasks coming up today. How do you think you will use LLMs?
    2. PM: PM: How did you use LLMs today? Were there any moments that stood out to you, e.g., successful usage, or frustrating usage?
1. Set up the email reminder automation in MS Workflows (or another tool of your choice). [Here is a tutorial video](https://www.loom.com/share/44d99fc6a1b140d18ba356520cf85d53). Make sure to test it. 
    1. In the email body, paste the link to your form for data collection.
2. Start data collection - complete your entries at the beginning and end of the days. 
3. It's okay to miss a day or to make up a PM entry the next AM - we can handle that in data cleaning before analysis.
4. We will discuss analysis around week 4.
