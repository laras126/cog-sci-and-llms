# Reading Reflections

Each week, there will be several readings that can support your understanding of the material. It is up to you how many you want to do - what is listed is probably more than is a reasonable time commitment for a 1-credit class.

So...what can you do to read more than you have time for? Read with LLMs! 

Yes _and_ we are going to stay on brand for the course and do this intentionally according to a specific structure for reading reflections, and we are going to collect some data to see how the LLM reading compares to the non LLM reading. 

For each week (starting week 2):

1. Choose two readings you are going to reflect on. 
1. Read one on your own and one with an LLM (see below ideas for this).
2. Record your reflection for each in the Canvas assignment.

### Reading with LLMs

There are many approaches - a few examples:
- Notebook LM to summarize the reading and quiz you on it (though this ends up quizzing on vocab, which is not necessarily the point of the paper)
- You can upload the paper to an LLM and ask it to walk you through the content by connecting it to a topic you already know
- You can simply ask for a summary and the main points

The goal of doing this is to see the difference in how you experience the readings with an LLM without, and how much you retain from the readings.
