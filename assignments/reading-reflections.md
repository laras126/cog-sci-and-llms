## How to Read

Some of the articles assigned will be old, long, and generally just hard to read. When reading academic articles, going from beginning to end and **reading every word from top to bottom is typically _not_ the best approach.** That said, I will do that if it is a very important reading that I am interested in.

[This article](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs114/reading-keshav.pdf) for Week 2 reviews good tips for reading papers.

I will also add some of my own approaches:
- Before reading:
    - Consider the date, what was going on in the world at that time?
    - Look up the author, what might their perspective be?
    - Look up where the paper was published or look up the book - what is the general topic or area?
- When you need a plainer language description:
    - Look up the paper on Google Scholar and look at papers that cited it. Look for a more recent paper and find where they cited it in the paper, and they maybe provided a big picture description of it.
- If there are general concepts or language referenced you don't know about:
    - Look up concepts on Youtube or, of course, use an LLM for explainers

Bascically, reading is not just reading words - it is bringing all kinds of sources in to help you understand the content. _However_, as we will learn, the struggle is often where the learning happens, so it is important to also struggle with the reading.

## Reading with LLMs

There are many approaches - a few examples:
- You can upload the paper to an LLM and ask it to walk you through the content by connecting it to a topic you already know
- Ask it to walk you through the main concepts as a story
- Tell it to ask you to reflect critically on the reading
- Tell the LLM you will teach it the concepts and ask it to correct you
- Notebook LM to summarize the reading, make a podcast about it, or quiz you on it (though this ends up quizzing on vocab, which is not necessarily the point)
- You can simply ask for a summary and the main points

The goal of doing this is to see the difference in how you experience the readings with an LLM without, and how much you retain from the readings.
