The PMM study is a way to see how your ideas about LLMs have evolved over the course of the seminar. 

**Active:** Week 1 (pre) and Week 9 (post)

## Overview

A Personal Meaning Map is a method for capturing what you know and how you think about a concept, before and after an experience. You will complete one at the start of the seminar and one at the end to document how your understanding of LLMs and your own cognition has changed.

## Steps

1. Watch this video about creating a personal meaning map and how to record the content.
2. Week 1: Upload your initial map and your reflection transcript to Canvas. 
2. Week 9: Upload your final map and your reflection transcript to Canvas. 
3. Include a comparison of the pre/post maps in your final presentation.

### Pre (Week 1)

Complete your PMM map at the start of the seminar, before you have done any readings or watched any course content. The idea is to capture what you currently know and think about LLMs and your own cognition.

### Post (Week 9-10)

Complete a second PMM map near the end of the seminar. Do a short analysis comparing the two - what changed, what stayed the same, and what surprised you - and report on it in 1 slide in your final findings presentation.

## What to Submit

- PMM pre map and transcript (week 1)
- PMM post map and transcript (week 9)
- Comparative analysis (week 9)

## Gen AI Guidelines
Don't use LLMs during making your maps or transcript, but you could use it as a thinking partner during the comparative analysis AFTER you have spent time on the analysis yourself.
