# Gen AI Policy

## Highlights

- Gen AI use is a core part of the course — you will use it intentionally and reflectively, not just incidentally
- You may be assigned to use or not use gen AI for a given task — if told not to use it, you are trusted to follow that instruction
- Honesty about your gen AI use is essential to the seminar's research integrity and your own learning
- **Always disclose** gen AI use: describe what you used it for, what the experience was like, and how it shaped the result

## Longer Version

This seminar is all about using gen AI and understanding its impacts on cognition through experimentation and reflection. This will involve a comparison of experiences and work outputs completed with gen AI vs. without. In some parts of the seminar, students will be assigned a role to use or not use gen AI in a task. If instructed not to use it, they will be trusted to perform the task without gen AI. Honesty from students regarding their use of gen AI is critical to the success of the seminar and students' own learning.

Regardless of whether gen AI use is assigned or self-directed, **you must always disclose your use of gen AI**. This means describing how you used it, how you felt about the experience, and how you think it impacted the outcome.

For example:
- I used Claude Code in creating this page/website - I fed it content I already wrote and asked it to fill in the related areas on the site, and to expand on the content in particular ways, e.g., add the highlights section below.
- I felt good about this - I genuinely think it took less time, and I could easily update multiple areas with a single command, e.g., adding a "Readings" section to the blocks on the calendar page. Since I had already done the thinking, this helped with operations, though when I asked it to write a script for showing the most recent calendar event, I kind of missed writing the code myself because I used to enjoy that.
- Using Claude Code impacted the outcome in that I think it sped up my work, and it also made it so that I did not have to write any code...this could have been an experience where I refreshed my coding skills, but it was not.
