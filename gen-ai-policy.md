# Gen AI Policy

This seminar is all about using gen AI and understanding its impacts on cognition through experimentation and reflection. So, there are some guidelines, and its up to you to follow them to benefit from the seminar experience.

- Use gen AI (namely LLMs) **intentionally and refectively, not automatically**, according to guidelines as we go through them in the seminar, for example, if you find yourself turning to LLMs immediately without thinking, think about that.
- In parts of the seminar, you may be directed to use or not use gen AI for a task -- following that guide is a key part of benefitting from the content.
- Honesty about your gen AI use is essential to your research integrity and your own learning -- **always disclose** gen AI use: describe what you used it for, what the experience was like, and how it shaped the result

Example disclosure (me, Lara, from making this website):
- **What I used and what for:** I used Claude Code in creating this page/website - I fed it content I already wrote and asked it to fill in the related areas on the site, and to expand on the content in particular ways, e.g., add the highlights section below.
- **How did it go/how did I feel about it:** I felt good about this - I genuinely think it took less time, and I could easily update multiple areas with a single command, e.g., adding a "Readings" section to the blocks on the calendar page. Since I had already done the thinking, this helped with operations, though when I asked it to write a script for showing the most recent calendar event, I kind of missed writing the code myself because I used to enjoy that.
- **How LLM use influenced the output:**  Using Claude Code impacted the outcome in that I think it sped up my work, and it also made it so that I did not have to write any code...this could have been an experience where I refreshed my coding skills, but it was not.
